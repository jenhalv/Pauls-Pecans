Pecan Producers purpose of grafting

Grafting a pecan tree is a special technique that helps with variety selection and rapid production. By grafting the scionwood/graftwood from a mature, producing parent tree onto the trunk (root system/rootstock) of another tree, the newly grafted tree will produce nuts that are identical to the parent tree. The newly grafted tree will also begin producing pecans at a much faster rate. A native tree may not produce pecans for 15 years, but a younger grafted tree can begin producing pecans within a few years because it takes on the producing traits of the parent tree!
