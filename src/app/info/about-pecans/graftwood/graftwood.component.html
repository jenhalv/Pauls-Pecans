Collecting graftwood

  • Graftwood (or scionwood) is a shoot or twig collected from your desired variety parent/superior pecan tree.
  • Determine the type of pecan you want to produce based on the:
    • size of the pecan,
    • the quality of the nut meat,
    • and how easily it cracks in a mechanical cracker.
  • For ideal graftwood, choose small limbs from trees that have two-year growth.
  • Bundle up to cut graftwood in late February/early March, the dormant season.
  • Have small and large pruners on hand to cut limbs.
  • Cut a variety of graftwood sizes (from pencil thin to the size of a fat marker: 1/4-inch to 1/2-inch in diameter) to fit different tree trunks.
  • Cut the graftwood in lengths of 8-10 inches, looking for at least three buds on your graftwood to indicate where new growth will begin.
  • Refer to the Storing Graftwood section of our website to properly protect your graftwood.

Storing graftwood

  • Graftwood should be processed the same day it is collected to prevent the graft sticks from drying out.
  • You will need:
    • graftwood pieces
    • candle wax
    • damp paper towels
    • baggies
    • sharpie/marker
    • refrigerator
  • After dipping the freshly cut ends of your graftwood in candle wax, wrap a small bundle in a damp paper towel and store in a baggie.
  • The variety of pecan and the current year should be labeled on the baggie.
  • Refrigerate the bags of graftwood until late April/early May.

  • Do not place graftwood in a refrigerator that also stores fruit (especially apples). Fruit gives off ethylene gas that can kill your graftwood.
