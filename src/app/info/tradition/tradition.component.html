{{photo.caption}} Paul "Pop" Droz began grafting & growing pecan trees (pronounced "puh-KAHN" in our neck of the woods‌) in 1972 with his father, Glenn Droz.  Together they planted hundreds of trees from seed and grafted numerous native trees to produce more plump, meaty nuts.  Paul continues harvesting pecans & grafting trees on his property and teaching his children and grandchildren the ins and outs of the pecan farming business.

Paul has around 3,000 pecan trees on his property, ranging from less than a year to over 150 years old!  The trees grow best in rich bottomland soils.  The Droz property in Rich Hill is adjacent to the Marais des Cygnes River and has an abundance of pecan, hickory, walnut, and other native Missouri trees. ‌

His oldest daughter, Jennifer, developed this website to showcase information she has collected from Paul detailing the pecan grafting process.  To learn more, visit our About page. {{card.caption}}
